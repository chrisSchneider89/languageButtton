The Schneider family has a disabled child—no arms, no legs, no torso, just a head.

Christmas Eve: The head is lying on the sofa, whining, “Daddy, Daddy, when are we finally going to open presents? When can I open my present?” Daddy Schneider: “No, my dear head, we’re waiting a little longer!”

Ten minutes later: Head: “Dad, please, please...”

Dad: “Okay, go ahead.”

So the head rolls off the sofa, bangs into the Christmas tree, finally rips open the package by moving its mouth wildly back and forth, and finally says: “Ooooooh, another hat...”
